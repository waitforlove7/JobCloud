CRISP-DM
Cross-industry standard process for data mining (CRISP-DM) breaks the process of
data mining into 6 major phases:
1. Business Understanding 2. Data Understanding 3. Data Preparation
4. Modeling 5. Evaluation 6. Deployment

Step 3 – Data Preparation
Transforming real-world data into well-formed data:
Data Consolidation: Collect, select, and integrate data.
Data Cleaning: Impute missing values, reduce noise, handle inconsistencies.
Data Transformation: Normalize, discretize, or construct new attributes.
Data Reduction: Reduce dimensionality, balance skewed data.

Steps 4 & 5 – Modeling & Evaluation
4. Model Building: Apply and compare analytical techniques.
Techniques vary based on data form and task (e.g., numeric constraints, classification
vs. regression).
5. Testing and Evaluation: Assess model performance.
Regression: Measure distance from actual values.
Classification: Evaluate accuracy and error rates.
We typically divide data into training/testing sets and use K-Fold Cross
Validation.

Step 6 – Deployment to MLOps
Traditionally, deployment involved generating a static report or model.
Today, this phase has evolved into Machine Learning Operations (MLOps).
CI/CD/CT: Continuous Integration, Continuous Deployment, and Continuous Training
to automatically update models as new data arrives.
Preventing Model Drift: Ensuring models don't degrade over time due to shifting real-
world conditions.
Observability: Real-time monitoring of model decisions and latency in production
environments.

F-Statistics
F-Statistics is another test to determine whether there is a relationship between the
response and the predictors.
Value close to 1  no relationship between the response and predictors.
Value much larger than 1  likely to find relationship between the response and
predictors.
More importantly, look at the p-value for the F-statistic to determine if it is significant.
